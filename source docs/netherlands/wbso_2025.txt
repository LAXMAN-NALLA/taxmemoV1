WBSO (Wet Bevordering Speur- en Ontwikkelingswerk) R&D Tax Credit Rates 2025

The WBSO (Research and Development Tax Credit Act) provides tax benefits for companies engaged in research and development activities in the Netherlands.

2025 WBSO Tax Credit Rates:

For the first 350,000 euros of R&D wage costs:
- Tax credit rate: 32% (increased from previous years)
- This applies to the first tranche of qualifying R&D wage costs

For wage costs above 350,000 euros:
- Tax credit rate: 16%
- This applies to the second tranche of qualifying R&D wage costs

Key Points for 2025:
- The threshold of 350,000 euros is indexed annually
- The tax credit reduces the wage tax and social security contributions payable
- Companies can claim WBSO for both in-house R&D and contracted R&D work
- The credit applies to wages of employees directly involved in R&D activities

Eligibility Requirements:
- R&D activities must involve technical-scientific research
- Activities must be innovative and involve technical uncertainty
- The work must be systematic and aimed at acquiring new knowledge
- Both profit and non-profit organizations can apply

Application Process:
- Companies must apply for WBSO before starting R&D activities
- Applications are submitted to the Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO)
- The WBSO declaration must be submitted within 5 months after the end of the calendar year
- Advance rulings are available for certainty on eligibility

Interaction with Other Tax Benefits:
- WBSO can be combined with the Innovation Box regime
- The tax credit is applied before calculating corporate income tax
- For startups, additional benefits may be available under the starter's deduction

Important Notes for 2025:
- The rates have been increased to support innovation post-COVID
- Small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) receive the same rates as larger companies
- The credit is calculated on gross wage costs including social security contributions
- Documentation requirements are strict - companies must maintain detailed records of R&D activities
