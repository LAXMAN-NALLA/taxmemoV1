Netherlands Corporate Income Tax (Vennootschapsbelasting - VPB) Rates 2025

The Corporate Income Tax (VPB) in the Netherlands applies to resident companies and non-resident companies with a permanent establishment in the Netherlands.

2025 Corporate Income Tax Rates:

Standard Rate Structure:
- First bracket (profits up to 200,000 euros): 19%
- Second bracket (profits above 200,000 euros): 25.8%

These rates apply to taxable profits after deductions and exemptions.

Key Changes for 2025:
- The 19% rate for the first 200,000 euros remains unchanged from 2024
- The top rate of 25.8% for profits above 200,000 euros is maintained
- The threshold of 200,000 euros is indexed annually for inflation

Tax Base:
- Resident companies: Taxed on worldwide income
- Non-resident companies: Taxed only on Dutch-source income and income from a permanent establishment in the Netherlands
- Taxable profit is calculated according to Dutch tax law, with adjustments for book profit

Deductions and Exemptions:
- Participation exemption (deelnemingsvrijstelling): Dividends and capital gains from qualifying participations are exempt
- Innovation Box: Profits from qualifying R&D activities can be taxed at a reduced rate of 9%
- Loss carry-forward: Unlimited carry-forward of losses, subject to certain restrictions
- Loss carry-back: One year carry-back available for losses up to 1 million euros

Small Business Considerations:
- The progressive rate structure benefits small businesses with profits under 200,000 euros
- Combined with WBSO tax credits, effective tax rate can be significantly reduced
- Startups may benefit from additional deductions and exemptions

Fiscal Unity (Fiscale Eenheid):
- Groups of companies can form a fiscal unity for tax purposes
- Allows for consolidation of profits and losses within the group
- Specific conditions must be met (at least 95% ownership, etc.)

International Aspects:
- The Netherlands has an extensive tax treaty network
- EU directives apply (Parent-Subsidiary Directive, Interest and Royalties Directive)
- Transfer pricing rules apply to transactions with related parties
- CFC (Controlled Foreign Company) rules may apply

Payment and Filing:
- Corporate income tax returns must be filed within 5 months after the end of the financial year
- Advance tax payments are required quarterly
- Final assessment is issued by the Dutch Tax Authority (Belastingdienst)

Penalties and Interest:
- Late filing penalties apply
- Interest is charged on overdue tax payments
- Criminal penalties for tax evasion

Special Regimes:
- Innovation Box: 9% rate on qualifying innovation profits (subject to conditions)
- Energy Investment Allowance (EIA): Additional deduction for energy-efficient investments
- Environmental Investment Allowance (MIA): Deduction for environmentally friendly investments

Comparison with Previous Years:
- 2024 rates: 19% (first 200,000) and 25.8% (above 200,000)
- 2023 rates: 19% (first 200,000) and 25.8% (above 200,000)
- Rates have been stable, with the threshold being adjusted for inflation

Effective Tax Rate Considerations:
- The statutory rate is 19-25.8%, but effective rates can be lower due to:
  - Deductions and exemptions
  - Innovation Box regime (9% on qualifying profits)
  - WBSO tax credits
  - Other investment allowances

Important Notes for 2025:
- No major rate changes announced for 2025
- The 200,000 euro threshold may be adjusted for inflation
- Companies should monitor for any legislative changes
- Consider the interaction with other taxes (dividend withholding tax, etc.)
